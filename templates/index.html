Relationship between Population and Temperature. A Visualisation

By Arshad Sheikh, Belinda Achuo, Muhammed Amjad & Sam Stafford

The effects of global warming are already bringing harm to human communities and the natural world. Further temperature rises will have a devastating impact and more action on greenhouse gas emissions is urgently required. Multiple factors contribute to climate change, and multiple actions are needed to address it. The number of people on our planet is one of those factors. Every additional person increases carbon emissions — the rich far more than the poor — and increases the number of climate change victims – the poor far more than the rich. The charts below represent a relationship between population changes and temperature variations; a prime factor signifying global warming, over 5 decades of a country. The difference in population (in millions) and temperature (in degree celsius) between each decade, for a country can be seen on the charts and inferred if an increase in population has any effect on temperature and ultimately global warming. The charts do not show how much the population or temperature has increased or decreased over the years, but how much variation has occurred between the decades. This draws a better picture in visualising the population/temperature relationship.
